Nucleic Acids

Nucleic Acids: Key Facts

  • Key Elements: C, H, O, N, P
  • Monomer forms: Nucleotides
  • Polymer forms: DNA and RNA
  • Function: Stores genetic information
  • If all of the DNA in a average adult human body was stretched out, it would be twice the diameter of the solar system.
  • Each human cell has approximately 6 billion nucleotides
Nucleotides
  • Each human cell has approximately 6 billion nucleotides
  • Each Nucleotide has three parts: A phosphate, a nitrogenous base, and a 5 carbon sugar named Deoxyribose, or a Ribose
  • Nucleotides in the form of ATP are what directly power cells
Bases
  • There are four types of DNA bases, Adenine (A), Guanine (G), Cytosine (C) and Thymine (T)
  • A bonds with T. C bonds with G (Apple Trees, and put the Car in the Garage).
  • In RNA, Uracil (U) takes the place of Thymime (T)
RNA
  • While DNA gets the spotlight, RNA plays a far more active role a cell
  • There are three major types of RNA transfer RNA (tRNA), messenger RNA (mRNA) and ribosomal RNA (rRNA)
