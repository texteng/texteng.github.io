Biomolecules

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates: Key Facts

  • Key Elements: C, H, O (In a 1:2:1 Ratio)
  • Monomer forms: Monosaccharides
  • Polymer forms: Polysaccharides
  • Functions: Main source of energy of life, and the key structural molecule in plants
  • Additional facts: While the energy storage froms of Carbohydrates get most of the attention, cellulose is the most abundant biomolecule on the planet. It is found in paper, and in cotton (something you are probably wearing at the moment)
  • Many carbohydrates end with the suffix -ose
Key Monosaccharides
  • Glucose: The major form of energy
  • Fructose: A sugar often found in fruits
  • Galactose: A key molecule in lactose, milk sugar
  • Deoxyribose/Ribose: A key sugar found in DNA and RNA
Disaccharides
  • Carbohydrates made of only two monomers
  • Sucrose: (Glucose + Fructose): table sugar
  • Lactose: (Glucose + Galactose): milk sugar
Polysaccharides
  • Carbohydrates made of many monomers
  • Starch: The major storage form of sugars in plants
  • Glycogen: The major storage form of sugars in animals
  • Cellulose: the major structural molecule in plants
