Description

  • Complete or partial inablity to hear sounds
  • 15 percent of people over 18 report some hearing loss (Nordqvist, 2018)

Types of Hearing Loss

  • Prelingual - Inability to hear before language is learned. Usually leads to delayed language and social development (Nordqvist, 2018)
  • Post-lingual - Losing hearing after one has learned language- Usually a gradual loss over time (Nordqvist, 2018)

Levels of Deafness

  • Mild - Can only hear sounds between 25 and 29 decibels (dB) (Nordqvist, 2018)
  • Moderate - Can only hear sounds between 40 and 69 dB (Nordqvist, 2018)
  • Severe - Can only hear sounds above 70 to 89 dB (Nordqvist, 2018)
  • Profound - Cannot hear a sound below 90dB (Nordqvist, 2018)

Challenges

  • The increasing multimedia nature of the web makes the web increasingly more difficult (Swierenga, 2002).
  • Unable to hear the audio "beeps" of a computer
  • Until recently, computers were not good interpreters of sign language (Jensen & Øvad, 2016)
  • Video conferencing has proven to be difficult between deaf and non deaf users. Translation of sign language remains difficult (Trindade, et al., 2012)

Software Accomodations

  • Closed captioning on video sites like youTube. Some have auto-generated closed captions.
  • Google voice can convert voice messages into text
  • Smartphone speech to sign language translators

Hardware Accomodations

  • Phones are adding vibrating and visual alerts

Developer Tools

  • Provide muitiple contact options other then phone or a video chat option
  • Provide text transcripts for longer videos possibly using a transcription service
