Description

  • "Any condition that impedes an individual's ability to execute typical daily activities due to visual loss.” (Hakobyan, Lumsden, O’Sullivan, & Bartlett, 2013)
  • 20% of people over the age of 75 years in the UK have sight loss (Leuthold, Bargas-Avila, & Opwis, 2008)
  • The definition of being legally blind is an eyesight of 20/200 or worse(Swierenga, 2002)
  • Globally there are 285 million visually impaired people. (Hakobyan, Lumsden, O’Sullivan, & Bartlett, 2013)

Challenges

  • Mobile devices are “no longer just telephones, but now offer an impressive cluster of features in a compact, portable form” (Hakobyan, Lumsden, O’Sullivan, & Bartlett, 2013)
  • Touchscreens do not have any tactile way of telling what I going on.
  • Computers are based of Graphic User Interfaces (GUI) which can't be seen (Leuthold, Bargas-Avila, & Opwis, 2008)
  • In order to navigate a website, a person who is blind must form a "mental model of the navigation space based solely on the linear representation of navigation items and audible cues." (Leuthold, Bargas-Avila, & Opwis, 2008)
  • Most websites are designed for 20/40 corrected vision (Swierenga, 2002)
  • While videos have captions for those hard of hearing, most videos do not have any form of audio description for the blind

Developer Tips

  • Use descriptive "alt tags" for images. For example, instead of using the alt tag "car" use "Camry in showroom"
  • Because the blind are using linear mental models consistancy is something to keep in mind. (Leuthold, Bargas-Avila, & Opwis, 2008)
  • For video, try creating a version with audio descriptions like the one at the bottom of the page
